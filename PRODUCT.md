# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

SignBox documentation serves business initiators who create and track signing processes, external recipients who authenticate and sign with national e-ID methods, and administrators who configure document profiles, contacts, templates, and process visibility. The primary audience includes HR, legal, procurement, finance, public-sector, and enterprise teams evaluating or learning the service.

## Product Purpose

The site explains how TrustLynx SignBox manages legally valid electronic-signature workflows from document upload and recipient routing through signing, status tracking, and delivery of a validated signed document. Success means a first-time visitor can understand the two-portal model, choose parallel or sequential routing, and recognize realistic organizational use cases without needing product-specific prior knowledge.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, precise, and enterprise-ready. The experience should feel calm, premium, and operationally credible, with the seriousness expected from a fintech and trust-service product.

## Anti-references

Avoid childish or cartoon-like visuals, playful bouncing, decorative motion without workflow meaning, abstract card-and-label diagrams, excessive glow, cluttered dashboards, fake company or personal data, and generic SaaS component grids that do not resemble a real signing product.

## Design Principles

1. Show the real workflow: use recognizable product surfaces, document states, recipients, authentication, and validation results.
2. Make trust legible: communicate secure handoffs, controlled routing, status visibility, and validated outcomes.
3. Prefer operational clarity: motion must explain sequence or state rather than decorate the page.
4. Preserve enterprise restraint: use concise copy, measured density, consistent hierarchy, and selective semantic color.
5. Teach at a glance: key distinctions should remain understandable without relying on long explanatory paragraphs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain readable text and semantic labels, sufficient color contrast consistent with the existing AA-oriented theme, responsive layouts across desktop, tablet, and mobile, and complete support for `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color is supplementary to readable status text and icons; reduced-motion views show the final completed state without looping or moving packets.
